About the Project

The Barton Plants database provides information about 259 plants both native to Texas and endemic to the Barton Creek watershed in Austin. The site is designed to serve anybody interested in learning more about one of Austin’s most beautiful and diverse landscapes, from gardeners, botanists, and aspiring naturalists to locavores and curious hikers and joggers.

Information in the database, which was built in April 2015 and should be used for purely educational purposes, was compiled from a variety of sources, though its foundation is Ann Bond’s Native Plant Society of Texas, Austin Chapter, survey of plants within the Barton Creek Habitat Preserve.

We do not accept responsibility or liability for any ingested plant material. Rather, we urge caution before eating anything listed as edible in the database and also recommend consulting a more authoritative source, such as Delena Tull’s Edible and Useful Plants of the Southwest: Texas, New Mexico, and Arizona: Revised Edition (University of Texas Press, 2013), before doing so.
